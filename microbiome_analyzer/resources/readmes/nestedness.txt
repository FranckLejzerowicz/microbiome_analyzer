In microbial ecology, this concept helps reveal non-random structures in community composition across environments (e.g., ordered extinctions or colonisation sequences). It allows to measure the NODF metric (Nestedness based on Overlap and Decreasing Fill) and an associated statistical significance based on randomization and a standardized effect size, as a quantification of the degree of nestedness in microbial presence-absence or abundance data. It also allows to plot the presence-absences after ordering of the samples according to richness and of the features according to prevalence, with coloring of samples based on metadata variable as specific in the configuration file passed to `microbiome_analyzer` with the `-ntd` option. For NOFD, the randomization can fix the species totals (row sums) and randomizes the sites uniformly ("fixedequiprobable") or fix the site totals (column sums) and randomizes the species uniformly ("equiprobablefixed"), to determine if the nested structure is due to constraints on species distributions, site capacities, or both, compared to random expectations. There are also three modes of calculating nestedness: (1) `betweenEachPairOfTypes`: to assess pairwise nestedness patterns, between each pair of predefined types (e.g., soil type) for fine-scale analysis of how nestedness varies across different group combinations; (2) `withinEachType`: to assess whether the structure is nested inside each category (e.g., one soil type) for determining if sub-communities independently show nested patterns; (3) `overall`: global nestedness of the dataset.",
